# Mist, Pearl, and Snowdrop

## Book Details

Working title: **Mist, Pearl, and Snowdrop**

Series label: **Book One**

Main character: **Snowdrop**, a small white cat with bright blue eyes.

Snowdrop's sister: **Frost**, a pale white cat with calm whiskers and gentle paws.

Kittens Snowdrop takes care of: **Mist**, a gray and white kitten with amber eyes, and **Pearl**, a white kitten with green eyes. Mist and Pearl are three weeks old when Frost brings them to Snowdrop, and Snowdrop takes care of them until they are two months old.

Other cats: **Marmalade**, an orange cat; **Pepper**, a black cat; **Poppy**, a calico cat; and **Clover**, a small tabby cat.

Main ideas: Responsibility, patience, asking questions, and learning how to care for others.

Audience: Early elementary / early chapter book read-aloud.

Tone: Fun, cozy, question-filled, cat-centered, and adventurous.

## Visual Character Guide

Use the same cat designs in every picture so young readers can recognize them from page to page.

**Snowdrop** is a small adult white cat with short soft fur, bright blue eyes, a pink nose, upright ears, and a calm, watchful expression. She has no colored patches or stripes.

**Frost** is Snowdrop's sister. She is a slightly taller pale white adult cat with a quieter, older look, long calm whiskers, gentle paws, and faint silvery shading on her ears and tail so she is not confused with Snowdrop.

**Mist** is a gray and white male kitten with round amber eyes. He has a gray patch over one ear, soft gray tabby markings over his head and back, and a white chin, chest, belly, and paws. He keeps these markings as he grows from three weeks old to two months old.

**Pearl** is a white female kitten with green eyes, a pink nose, and soft round kitten features. She stays all white, with no gray or orange patches, as she grows from three weeks old to two months old.

**Marmalade** is a round orange tabby cat. **Pepper** is a black cat with one white front paw. **Poppy** is a calico cat with clear orange, black, and white patches. **Clover** is a small brown-gray tabby cat who often carries or guards the leopard-tail leaf.

Do not add extra cats unless the scene calls for them. Do not change eye colors, fur colors, markings, or relative ages between illustrations.

## Story Text

### Page 1

At dawn, when the sky was pale pink and the grass was still wet with dew, Snowdrop woke up in the warm little cat den at the end of the quiet lane.

The den was tucked behind a row of lavender bushes, under a sloping shed roof, beside a garden where bees slept in flowers and beetles marched like tiny soldiers.

Snowdrop stretched one white paw, then the other. Her fur was soft as a cloud, her steps were quiet as falling snow, and her eyes were bright, bright blue.

### Page 2

Snowdrop's blue eyes were famous among the cats of the lane.

When she looked at puddles, they seemed to sparkle. When she looked through tall grass, she could spot the smallest beetle, the sleepiest moth, and the sneakiest shadow.

"Snowdrop sees everything," Marmalade liked to say.

That was not exactly true. Snowdrop sometimes missed a rolling acorn. Sometimes she stepped on her own tail. Once, she tried to pounce on a leaf and landed nose-first in a flowerpot.

But Snowdrop noticed little things.

And little things often became big adventures.

### Page 3

The den had three blankets, one round window, one water bowl, and one very squeaky floorboard near the door.

Snowdrop knew the squeaky board well. If she stepped on the left side, it said eeek. If she stepped on the right side, it said eek-eek.

This morning, she stepped over it.

Outside, lavender nodded in the soft air. The garden smelled sweet and dusty. A blackbird hopped along the wall, pretending not to look for crumbs.

### Page 4

Snowdrop washed one ear, then the other.

She liked to begin the day neatly. A neat ear helped a cat listen. A neat whisker helped a cat think. A neat tail helped a cat look calm even when a beetle marched over her paw.

The beetle marched over her paw.

Snowdrop lifted her foot and waited.

"Excuse me," she said.

The beetle did not answer. It had places to go.

### Page 5

Marmalade arrived exactly when the sun reached the warm stone.

Marmalade was orange, round, cheerful, and almost always hungry. He had a habit of arriving whenever something interesting might happen, especially if breakfast might happen too.

"Good morning," he said. "Are you having an adventure yet?"

"Not yet," said Snowdrop.

"Could you hurry?" asked Marmalade. "I am free until breakfast."

"Adventures do not usually come when called," said Snowdrop.

Marmalade sat down. "I can wait loudly."

### Page 6

Pepper slipped through the gap under the fence like a shadow with whiskers.

Pepper was black from nose to tail, except for one white front paw that looked as if he had stepped in cream.

"You are both too loud," Pepper said.

"I have only said eight things," Marmalade objected.

"That is seven things too many before breakfast."

Snowdrop blinked her blue eyes.

Poppy came after Pepper, bright calico patches flashing in the sun. Clover followed last, carrying a long dry leaf in her mouth like a prize.

### Page 7

"What is that?" asked Poppy.

Clover dropped the leaf proudly. "A leopard tail."

Pepper sniffed it. "It is a leaf."

"A leaf can be a leopard tail if you carry it correctly," said Clover.

"I see it," said Snowdrop. "It curves just like one."

"It is still a leaf," said Pepper.

"A very leafy leaf," said Poppy.

Clover picked it up again and marched around the flowerpots with her chin high.

### Page 8

The cats spent the morning making the garden into a kingdom.

The flowerpots became towers. The old watering can became a cave. The garden wall became a mountain. The lavender bushes became a purple forest full of invisible mice.

Invisible mice were difficult to catch because nobody could agree where they were.

Marmalade declared himself king of the sunny stone.

Poppy declared herself queen of the fence.

Clover declared herself captain of the leopard-tail leaf.

Pepper declared that everyone was being too loud.

### Page 9

Snowdrop declared nothing at all.

She watched the sky brighten from dawn to morning. She listened to the bees hum awake. She saw a trail of ants carrying a crumb around a pebble and wondered why they did not simply climb over it.

The garden was ordinary.

The garden was also full of tiny happenings.

Snowdrop liked that best. A cat did not need a roaring river or a giant mountain to have an adventure.

Sometimes a leaf was enough.

### Page 10

By noon, the warmest patch of sunlight had moved beside the cat den. The morning dew was gone, the stones were warm, and the garden smelled like sun, grass, and lavender.

The cats began a game called Pounce on the Shadow Without Pouncing on Your Friend, which was much harder than it sounded.

Marmalade pounced on Pepper's shadow.

Pepper pounced on Poppy's shadow.

Poppy pounced on Clover's leopard-tail leaf.

Clover shouted, "My leopard tail!"

Snowdrop was about to pounce when she heard soft pawsteps behind the lavender.

### Page 11

The pawsteps stopped.

Snowdrop lifted her head.

"Did anyone else hear that?" she asked.

Marmalade froze with one paw still on Pepper's shadow. Poppy climbed down from the fence. Clover tucked her leaf under one paw.

Pepper listened.

The lavender branches trembled, though there was no wind.

"Someone is there," Pepper said.

Snowdrop walked toward the bushes. She did not rush. Rushing made little creatures hide, and it made tired cats more tired.

### Page 12

"Hello?" Snowdrop called softly.

The lavender smelled stronger where the branches had been brushed aside. A few purple flowers fell onto the path.

For a moment, no one answered.

Then a pale face appeared between the stems.

Snowdrop knew that face. She knew the calm whiskers, the gentle paws, and the faint silvery shading on the ears.

"Frost?" she said.

Frost stepped into the garden, and the whole game stopped.

### Page 13

Frost was Snowdrop's sister.

She was pale white, like moonlight on snow, with a quieter, older look. Usually Frost moved as if she had all the time in the world.

Today she looked tired.

Her fur was smooth, but her steps were slow. Her eyes were kind, but they kept glancing down beside her paws.

Snowdrop followed Frost's gaze.

Beside Frost were two very small kittens.

### Page 14

One kitten was gray and white with round amber eyes. He had a gray patch over one ear, soft gray tabby markings over his head and back, and a white chin that made him look surprised.

The other kitten was white with green eyes, a pink nose, and paws that seemed too big for her body.

They were only three weeks old.

The gray and white kitten leaned against Frost's front leg.

The white kitten tried to sit down and accidentally rolled onto her side.

### Page 15

Marmalade whispered, "They are very small."

"I can hear whispers," said Pepper.

"I know," Marmalade whispered back.

Poppy's eyes went soft. Clover forgot her leopard-tail leaf completely.

Snowdrop did not move closer yet. The kittens were staring at everything: the lavender, the flowerpots, Marmalade's orange belly, Pepper's white paw, and Snowdrop's bright blue eyes.

The white kitten blinked at Snowdrop.

"Blue," she squeaked.

### Page 16

"Yes," said Snowdrop. "Blue."

The gray and white kitten made a tiny sound too, though it was difficult to tell whether he meant blue, hello, or I have a foot.

Frost lowered herself carefully onto the warm stone.

"Snowdrop," she said softly, "I need your help."

Snowdrop stepped closer then.

Frost did not often ask for help. She gave directions. She found lost paths. She knew which roofs were safe and which dogs barked only at clouds.

If Frost asked, it mattered.

### Page 17

Frost touched the gray and white kitten with her nose.

"This is Mist," she said. "He climbs before he checks how high things are."

Mist squeaked, as if this was an important thing to say.

Frost touched the white kitten next.

"And this is Pearl. She asks many questions, but she forgets where her paws are."

Pearl waved one paw in the air and blinked her green eyes at Snowdrop.

"Paw," Pearl said.

"That is yours," said Snowdrop.

### Page 18

Clover leaned toward Poppy. "Can kittens borrow leopard tails?"

"Not yet," Poppy whispered.

Marmalade leaned toward Pepper. "Can kittens eat breakfast?"

"Not yours," Pepper said.

Mist tried to climb Frost's front leg and slid back down. Pearl spotted Clover's leaf and began wobbling toward it.

Snowdrop stepped between Pearl and the leaf.

"Hello, Pearl," she said.

Pearl sat down suddenly, surprised by the floor.

### Page 19

Frost took a slow breath.

"I need you to take care of them until they are two months old," she said.

The garden went quiet.

Snowdrop looked at Mist. She looked at Pearl. Then she looked at Frost.

Taking care of kittens sounded simple until she actually saw the kittens. Mist was already trying to climb a pebble. Pearl was trying to nibble the end of Clover's leaf.

Even a dandelion looked tall beside them.

### Page 20

"They need warmth," Frost said. "They need soft voices. They need help finding safe places to walk. They need someone to keep them from tumbling into trouble."

"For five whole weeks?" asked Marmalade.

"Until they are two months old," said Frost.

Pepper looked at Mist, who had put one paw on the pebble and was considering the rest of the climb.

"That is a lot of tumbling," Pepper said.

Snowdrop stood a little taller.

"I can do that," she said.

### Page 21

Frost nuzzled Snowdrop's cheek.

"I know you can. And I will visit when I can."

Then Frost turned to the kittens. "Mist, Pearl, stay close to Snowdrop. Listen to her. She has very good eyes."

Mist stared at Snowdrop's blue eyes.

Pearl stared too.

"Blue," Pearl said again.

"Still blue," said Snowdrop.

Marmalade smiled. "I think they like her."

"Everyone likes Snowdrop," said Pepper.

"I like breakfast," said Marmalade.

### Page 22

Frost rested for a little while before she had to go.

Snowdrop brought her water. Poppy found a clean patch of shade. Clover placed the leopard-tail leaf nearby in case it was useful.

Mist curled against Frost's paw.

Pearl curled against Mist, then uncurled to look at Snowdrop.

Frost watched Snowdrop watching them.

"They are little," Frost said.

"I can see that," said Snowdrop.

"They will not stay little."

Snowdrop nodded, though the kittens looked as if they might stay tiny forever.

### Page 23

When Frost stood, Mist woke at once.

Pearl woke because Mist bumped her nose.

Frost bent and touched each kitten gently. "I will come back," she told them.

Mist squeaked.

Pearl pressed her pink nose against Frost's chin.

Snowdrop felt something settle inside her. It was not fear exactly. It was more like holding a bowl of water and trying not to spill any.

Frost stepped through the lavender and disappeared down the lane.

### Page 24

For one breath, no one moved.

Then Pearl began to follow Frost.

Snowdrop stepped in front of her.

"Not that way," Snowdrop said. "This way."

Pearl looked left. She looked right. She looked at Snowdrop's paws.

"Why?"

"Because Frost is coming back later, and you are staying here now."

Pearl considered this.

Mist considered the pebble again.

Marmalade whispered, "This adventure came when called after all."

### Page 25

Snowdrop led Mist and Pearl toward the cat den.

She walked slowly. Mist took three tiny steps for every one of hers. Pearl took two tiny steps, then one sideways step, then stopped to stare at a seed.

"Seed," Pearl said.

"Yes," said Snowdrop. "Seed."

"Why?"

"Because plants come from seeds."

Pearl stared harder.

Mist bumped into Snowdrop's back paw and sat down with a surprised squeak.

"We will learn walking first," Snowdrop said.

### Page 26

The den was quieter than the garden and warm from the noon sun.

Dust floated in the light from the round window. The three blankets waited in a soft heap. Outside, the other cats peeked in without crowding the doorway.

"This is the cozy corner," Snowdrop told the kittens. "Good for naps."

Mist stepped onto the blanket and sank down so far that only his ears showed.

Pearl waddled in a circle, then sat on Mist.

Mist squeaked.

Pearl squeaked back.

### Page 27

Snowdrop gently nudged Pearl to the side.

"First kitten rule," she said. "Do not sit on your brother unless he asks."

Pearl looked at Mist.

"Ask?" she said.

Mist blinked from the blanket valley.

"No," he squeaked.

Pearl nodded, very serious. Then she sat on the blanket beside him and immediately tipped over.

Marmalade stuck his head through the doorway.

"I can be a blanket," he offered.

"You can be quiet," said Pepper from outside.

### Page 28

The kittens did not nap.

They blinked. They wobbled. They sneezed. They tried to chase Snowdrop's tail even though Snowdrop moved it slowly and kindly for them.

Mist crawled under the blanket and became a mysterious blanket hill.

Pearl patted the hill.

"Mist?"

The hill squeaked.

Snowdrop lifted one blanket corner.

Mist looked pleased with himself.

"A hiding place is good," Snowdrop said. "A hiding place where you can still breathe is better."

### Page 29

Pearl found the water bowl.

She looked into it and seemed shocked to find another white kitten staring back.

"That kitten copies me," Pearl squeaked.

"That is your reflection," said Snowdrop.

Pearl touched the water with one paw. The reflection wiggled.

Pearl gasped. "Why does water make another kitten?"

"It does not make one," Snowdrop said. "It shows you one."

Pearl leaned close.

The other kitten leaned close too.

### Page 30

Mist crawled out from under the blanket and looked into the bowl.

"Friend?" he asked.

"Reflection," said Snowdrop again.

Mist touched the water. The gray and white kitten in the bowl broke into silver ripples.

Mist pulled back his wet paw and stared.

Pearl stared too.

Then both kittens looked at Snowdrop as if she had put magic in the bowl.

Snowdrop smiled. Pearl had found her first mystery, and Mist had splashed it.

### Page 31

Outside, Clover was trying to repair a bent corner of the leopard-tail leaf.

Poppy was telling her it still looked grand.

Pepper was pretending not to watch the doorway.

Marmalade was lying in the sun, but one ear pointed toward the den.

Snowdrop noticed all of them waiting.

The kittens were not only hers to watch. The whole garden had grown careful around them without anyone saying so.

That made Snowdrop feel less alone.

### Page 32

Snowdrop brought Mist and Pearl back outside for fresh air.

She chose the safest part of the garden, where the grass was short, the stones were flat, and the lavender bushes made a shady wall.

"Stay close to my side and the flowerpot," Snowdrop said.

Mist nodded.

Pearl nodded.

Then Mist marched toward a beetle.

Pearl marched toward a daisy.

They went in opposite directions.

Snowdrop hurried after Mist, then hurried after Pearl.

### Page 33

"Second kitten rule," Snowdrop said, catching her breath. "Adventure is better when everyone goes the same way."

Mist looked at Pearl.

Pearl looked at Mist.

Then they both looked in different directions again.

Marmalade rolled onto his side. "This rule may need practice."

"Most rules do," said Pepper.

Snowdrop nudged Mist away from the beetle with her nose, then nudged Pearl away from a sleepy bee.

The bee floated past them, heavy with pollen and not interested in kittens at all.

### Page 34

By afternoon, Mist and Pearl had learned the short path from the den to the lavender and back again.

They had also learned that Clover's leaf was not for chewing, Pepper's tail was not a toy, Poppy's fence was too high, and Marmalade's belly made an excellent pillow if he agreed first.

Snowdrop counted the lessons in her head.

Then she counted the kittens.

One gray and white.

One white.

Both safe.

For the first day, that was enough.

### Page 35

The next morning began with a sneeze.

Mist sneezed first.

Pearl sneezed because Mist had sneezed.

Marmalade woke outside the doorway and said, "Bless you, breakfast."

"We are not breakfast," said Pearl.

"I was speaking to breakfast in general," said Marmalade.

Snowdrop gave each kitten a gentle lick between the ears. Their fur smelled like blankets, milk, and lavender dust.

"Today," she said, "we learn the den."

Pearl looked around. "Why?"

"Because it is home for now."

### Page 36

The den had corners.

That was the first discovery.

Mist found the blanket corner, the water bowl corner, and the dusty corner behind the old basket.

Pearl found the round-window corner, even though a round window did not truly have corners.

"This corner has sky," Pearl said.

"That is a window," said Snowdrop.

"Why?"

"So light can come in."

Pearl blinked at the bright circle.

Mist tried to climb the basket and slid backward into the blanket.

### Page 37

Snowdrop showed them the squeaky floorboard.

"Step here," she said.

Mist stepped.

Eeek.

Pearl jumped.

Then Pearl stepped.

Eek-eek.

Mist jumped.

They stared at the board.

"Again?" asked Pearl.

"Only once more," said Snowdrop.

They stepped together.

Eeeeeek-eek.

Marmalade called from outside, "The floor is singing!"

"The floor is complaining," Pepper said.

### Page 38

When the kittens grew tired of the floorboard, Snowdrop showed them where the blankets folded.

"A blanket can be a nest," she said.

"Or a hill," Mist said.

"Or a cave," said Pearl.

"Yes," Snowdrop said. "But if it becomes a cave, tell me before you vanish inside."

Pearl nodded. Mist nodded.

Then both kittens vanished inside.

Snowdrop closed her eyes for one moment.

"Mist?"

Squeak.

"Pearl?"

"Cave," Pearl said proudly.

### Page 39

Poppy visited at midday with a small feather she had found near the fence.

"For gentle batting," she said.

"What is gentle?" asked Pearl.

Poppy placed the feather on the blanket and tapped it with one soft paw.

The feather spun.

Mist pounced so hard he rolled over it and into Pearl.

Pearl rolled into the blanket cave.

The feather floated down onto Snowdrop's nose.

Poppy smiled. "Gentle may need another try."

Snowdrop sneezed.

### Page 40

Clover visited with the leopard-tail leaf.

"They may look," Clover said, "but not bite."

Pearl looked.

Mist looked.

Marmalade looked too.

"That rule applies to everyone," Clover warned.

Marmalade shut his mouth.

Clover laid the leaf on the floor. She showed Mist how to walk around it without stepping on the crunchy edges. She showed Pearl how to touch it with one paw and not drag it away.

Pearl whispered, "Leaf."

Clover whispered back, "Leopard tail."

### Page 41

Pepper did not visit inside the den right away.

He sat by the doorway where one white paw rested in the sun.

Mist saw the paw.

He crept toward it.

"That is attached to Pepper," Snowdrop said.

Mist stopped.

Pepper opened one yellow eye.

Mist sat down just inside the doorway and looked at him.

Pepper looked back.

Nothing happened for a long moment.

Then Pepper curled his white paw closer.

Mist copied him with his own paw.

### Page 42

Marmalade offered to help by lying down beside the kittens like a soft orange hill.

"They can climb me," he said. "I am excellent for climbing."

Mist began climbing Marmalade's side. Pearl tried to climb his tail.

"Not the tail," said Marmalade quickly. "The tail is not a mountain."

Pearl pulled her paw back. "Sorry," she squeaked.

Marmalade blinked in surprise.

Snowdrop leaned close. "You noticed Marmalade did not like that," she said. "So you stopped and said sorry. That was kind."

### Page 43

Marmalade liked being a hill more than he expected.

Mist climbed to the top of his shoulder and looked around as if he had reached the garden wall.

Pearl climbed halfway, forgot what she was doing, and rested her chin on Marmalade's side.

"I am a mountain and a pillow," Marmalade said.

"You are also blocking the doorway," Pepper said.

"Mountains do that."

Snowdrop let the kittens climb only until their paws grew wobbly.

Then she guided them down for a rest.

### Page 44

Resting was not the same as sleeping.

Mist rested by chewing the edge of a blanket without actually chewing it.

Pearl rested by asking why the dust in the sun floated up instead of down.

Snowdrop did not know all the answers.

Sometimes she said, "I do not know."

Pearl seemed to like that answer too.

"Why do you not know?"

"Because no cat knows everything."

Pearl looked pleased.

"Then we can find out later."

### Page 45

That evening, Frost returned for a short visit.

Mist was asleep against Marmalade's front paw. Pearl was awake because she had heard Frost before anyone else did.

"Mama," Pearl squeaked, and tumbled toward the doorway.

Frost bent low and nuzzled her.

Mist woke, saw Frost, and tried to run before his back paws were ready.

Snowdrop caught him with one gentle paw.

Frost looked around the den.

"You made it cozy," she said.

"They made it busy," Snowdrop answered.

### Page 46

Frost stayed until the sky turned peach.

She listened while Snowdrop told her about the water bowl reflection, the singing floorboard, Marmalade Hill, and the leopard-tail rule.

Pearl interrupted often to say "why."

Mist interrupted once to climb Frost's shoulder and then decide he wanted down.

Frost helped him down.

"Five weeks is a long time," Snowdrop said softly.

"It is," said Frost. "And it is made of little days."

Snowdrop looked at the kittens.

This little day was almost done.

### Page 47

After Frost left, the den felt quiet.

Pearl pressed against Snowdrop's side.

Mist pressed against Pearl.

Marmalade snored outside the doorway. Pepper pretended he was not guarding it. Poppy slept on the fence. Clover slept with the leopard-tail leaf tucked under one paw.

Snowdrop listened to all of them.

The kittens' breathing was quick and soft.

The lavender brushed the wall.

The round window held one silver star.

Snowdrop stayed awake until both kittens were asleep.

### Page 48

In the morning, Mist found the squeaky floorboard again.

Pearl found the water bowl again.

Marmalade found breakfast.

Snowdrop found that she was tired, but not unhappy.

She washed each kitten's face. She checked their paws. She counted their whiskers without meaning to.

"What now?" asked Pearl.

Outside, a cloud slid over the sun.

The garden changed color.

Snowdrop looked at the sky.

"Now," she said, "we watch the weather."

### Page 49

Late in the afternoon, clouds gathered over the lane.

The bright garden turned silvery, and the air smelled like rain.

Mist shivered.

Pearl blinked up at the darkening sky.

"Is the sky growling?" Pearl asked.

A low rumble rolled far away.

Marmalade puffed up. "The sky is hungry."

"The sky is not hungry," said Pepper.

"Then why is it rumbling?" asked Clover.

Snowdrop looked at the clouds, then at the kittens.

"It is going to rain," she said. "We should go inside."

### Page 50

Before they could reach the den, one fat raindrop landed on Marmalade's nose.

"Cold!" he yelped.

Another raindrop splashed near Mist. Another dotted Pearl's white fur.

Then the rain began to patter on the leaves, the stones, the fence, and the shed roof.

Mist looked up with his mouth open.

"Do not drink the sky," Snowdrop said.

Mist closed his mouth.

Pearl laughed so hard she sat down in the wet grass.

### Page 51

The big cats hurried toward the den, but Mist stopped in the grass.

He had found a puddle.

In the puddle was a wobbly gray and white kitten with amber eyes.

"Friend?" Mist squeaked.

"Reflection," said Snowdrop, lifting him gently away just before he stepped in.

Mist looked back over her paw.

The puddle kitten looked back too.

Rain made circles all around its face.

Mist whispered, "Wet friend."

### Page 52

Pearl, meanwhile, had discovered that raindrops made the lavender bushes shake.

Every time a drop landed, a leaf bounced.

Bounce.

Bounce.

Bounce.

Pearl's green eyes grew wide. She patted one leaf, then another, then tried to crawl under the bush to find where the bouncing came from.

"Pearl," called Snowdrop.

Pearl did not answer.

Snowdrop's blue eyes searched the lavender shadows.

There was Pearl, half hidden under the leaves, very proud and very wet.

### Page 53

Snowdrop crawled in after Pearl.

Lavender brushed Snowdrop's ears. Raindrops slid down her nose.

"Third kitten rule," Snowdrop said gently. "If a leaf is dancing, you may watch it from somewhere dry."

Pearl looked at the bouncing leaves.

"Why?"

"Because wet kittens get cold."

Pearl thought about this. Then she sneezed.

"Cold," she agreed.

Snowdrop guided her out from under the bush and tucked her close against her chest.

### Page 54

Inside the den, the cats shook rain from their whiskers.

Marmalade shook rain from his ears.

Pepper shook rain from his one white paw.

Poppy shook rain from her calico tail.

Clover shook rain from the leopard-tail leaf, which had become a soggy leopard-tail leaf.

Clover stared at it.

"It is still grand," Poppy said quickly.

"It is floppy," said Clover.

"A floppy grand thing," said Snowdrop.

Clover brightened a little.

### Page 55

Snowdrop dried Mist and Pearl with the edge of the soft blanket.

Mist protested with tiny squeaks until he realized the blanket was warm.

Pearl tucked her wet nose under Snowdrop's chin.

"You smell like rain," Pearl whispered.

"So do you," said Snowdrop.

Rain tapped the roof in quick little paws.

Mist listened.

"Many paws," he said.

"Drops," said Snowdrop.

"Drop paws," said Pearl.

Marmalade yawned. "I prefer breakfast paws."

### Page 56

The rain grew louder.

It drummed on the shed roof and rushed from the edge in silver strings. The round window blurred until the garden looked like a painting made of water.

Pearl watched from the blanket.

"Where does rain sleep?" she asked.

"In clouds, maybe," said Poppy.

"In puddles after it falls," said Clover.

"In my fur," Marmalade complained.

Pepper sat near the doorway and watched the path.

Snowdrop watched Pepper.

He did not like storms, though he never said so.

### Page 57

Another rumble rolled over the lane.

Pearl pressed against Mist. Mist pressed against Snowdrop.

Marmalade pressed against everyone.

"The sky is only making noise," Snowdrop said.

"Loud noise," Pearl whispered.

"Yes."

Snowdrop curled her tail around both kittens. She did not tell them there was nothing to feel. She only stayed warm and steady beside them.

The thunder passed.

The rain kept tapping.

Mist breathed out slowly.

Pearl did too.

### Page 58

After a while, the rain softened.

The tapping became dripping. The dripping became plinking. The plinking became silence with little drops falling from leaves.

Snowdrop led the kittens to the doorway.

The garden had changed.

Every stone shone. Every lavender stem sparkled. Puddles held pieces of sky. The air smelled clean, green, and new.

Pearl whispered, "The garden washed itself."

"It did," said Snowdrop.

Mist looked at his paws.

"I washed too."

"Very much," said Snowdrop.

### Page 59

The cats stepped outside carefully.

### Page 63

Soon the garden was full of tiny questions.

Why did shadows stretch longer in the evening?

Why did Clover's leaf get crispy in the sun and floppy in the rain?

Why did Pepper pretend not to like games and then play anyway?

Pearl asked the most questions, but Mist wondered things too.

Snowdrop never snapped, "Stop asking." She said, "Let's look." Or, "Let's listen." Or, "Let's find out safely."

### Page 64

By the time Mist and Pearl were four weeks old, their paws were steadier.

They could follow Snowdrop from the den to the lavender bushes and back again.

Mist still bumped into things.

Pearl still asked why the things had been there.

Snowdrop still counted them whenever they passed behind a flowerpot.

One white.

### Page 65

One afternoon, Mist found Pepper sitting alone by the fence.

"Play?" Mist squeaked.

Pepper did not answer.

Mist looked at Snowdrop.

### Page 66

He did not smile.

He did not suddenly begin a game.

### Page 67

Snowdrop taught the kittens to listen to rain on the roof, bees in the flowers, wings in the bushes, and the soft warning sound grown cats made when a game became too rough.

"The fence creaks," said Mist.

"Marmalade snores," said Pearl.

"I do not snore," said Marmalade, waking himself up with a snort.

### Page 68

Marmalade smiled.

Snowdrop smiled too.

Later, Poppy showed the kittens how to listen for the flutter of wings before a bird appeared on the wall.

Clover showed them how a dry leaf made different sounds on stone, grass, and blanket.

Pepper showed them nothing on purpose, but Mist copied the way he tilted one ear toward far-off footsteps.

### Page 69

The kittens began to know the garden by sound.

Buzz meant bees.

Tap meant a beetle on the water bowl.

Scritch meant Clover was moving her leaf.

Thump meant Marmalade had rolled over.

Silence could mean many things.

It could mean Pepper was thinking.

It could mean Poppy had seen something from the fence.

It could mean Pearl had put both front paws into the water bowl and was waiting to see who noticed.

Snowdrop usually noticed.

### Page 70

One quiet afternoon, Snowdrop pretended not to notice for three whole breaths.

Pearl stood in the bowl with wet paws and a very serious face.

Mist stood beside her, not in the bowl, but clearly considering it.

"Why is water cold?" Pearl asked.

"Because it is not sun," said Marmalade.

"That is not an answer," said Pepper.

"It is a Marmalade answer," said Poppy.

Snowdrop lifted Pearl out and dried her paws.

"Cold water belongs in the bowl," she said.

Pepper closed both eyes.

Snowdrop pressed her whiskers together so she would not laugh.

Clover dragged the leopard-tail leaf across a stone.

Scritch.

Thump.

Tock.

Pearl answered first. "Everyone."

### Page 73

Pearl began to hear when Mist was about to climb.

There was a tiny determined huff before he put his paw up.

Snowdrop heard both sounds.

Sometimes she stopped the tumble or the climb.

Sometimes she let the kittens try, if the landing was soft and the height was small.

Learning was not only stopping.

Learning was also watching close.

### Page 74

Pearl showed her how the floorboard sang.

### Page 77

Book One begins in a lavender garden with Snowdrop, a quiet white cat with very bright blue eyes. At dawn, she expects an ordinary day with Marmalade, Pepper, Poppy, and Clover. But by noon, Snowdrop's sister Frost arrives with two three-week-old kittens: Mist, a gray and white kitten with amber eyes, and Pearl, a white kitten with green eyes.

Now Snowdrop has a big job. She must care for Mist and Pearl until they are two months old, keeping them warm, safe, happy, and out of trouble while they wobble, pounce, squeak, chase leaves, discover puddles, ask endless questions, and grow into steadier paws. A cat story about caring for two kittens.
